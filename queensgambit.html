Queen's Gambit

The Queen's Gambit is one of the most popular gambits played in Chess. A gambit is a chess opening in which a player sacrifices material with the goal of achieving better positioning. In this gambit White is trying to take control of the cetner of the board by shifting focus to the side.

White leads with pawn d4, black counters with pawn d5, and White follows with pawn c4. From here, black can choose to take the seemingly free piece with d5 takes c4. This position is the Queen's Gambit Accepted, and white has a variety of moves to play from here.

Queen's Gambit Accepted

Black takes the c4 pawn. White has a number of options.

Option 1: pawn to e3. This further defends the d4 pawn, and opens up the White bishop to attack the black c4 pawn.
